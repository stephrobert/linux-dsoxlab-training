# Context — the commands you must not think about

At the exam, `find`, `tar`, `cut`, `sort`, `ln`, `chown` and redirections are not
the subject: they are the **tools** you use to answer the real questions. If you
still hesitate on `-size +1M` or on `2>`, you burn minutes you do not have.

This drill is not a lesson — it is a **stopwatch**. 5 tasks, 20 minutes, no
hints. The same skills serve RHCSA and LFCS: these commands are strictly
identical on RHEL and Debian, which is why this drill is played on the
distribution of your choice.

What is being measured:

- **finding by criterion** (size), not by name;
- **aggregating text** (who appears most often);
- knowing the difference between a **hard link** and a **symbolic link**;
- **ownership and mode**, on a file that must not leak;
- **separating stdout from stderr**, not merging them.

Read the subject: `dsoxlab challenge drill-essential-commands`.
